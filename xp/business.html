Twisted Luxuries

A handmade accessories micro-business launched from scratch during recovery.

Problem

Shortly before beginning university, I sustained a fracture that required an extended period of bed rest. While recovering, I wanted to remain productive and financially independent rather than treating recovery as lost time. I also wanted to explore whether a personal creative skill could be converted into something with tangible value.

Actions Taken

I taught myself advanced stitching and handmade accessory creation during recovery and launched a small initiative called Twisted Luxuries. I designed and produced handmade items, developed a simple sales process, and began selling products within my college community after joining university.

Beyond generating income, I used the initiative to support fundraising efforts and community causes by directing portions of proceeds toward social-impact activities and charitable initiatives. I managed production, pricing, customer interactions, inventory planning, and promotion independently while balancing academic responsibilities.

Results

  • Successfully transformed a personal hobby into a revenue-generating micro-business before entering university
  • Built a small but consistent customer base within the campus community
  • Generated funds for social initiatives and charitable causes
  • Demonstrated ability to identify demand, create value, and execute independently with limited resources
  • Reinforced understanding of customer behaviour, pricing psychology, grassroots marketing, and building products people genuinely pay for
