02 — Concepts
Ideas I keep coming back to
Not shipped — documented thinking
These are product gaps I have identified and thought through to the point of knowing what is missing and why it persists. Not mockups, not promises — structured thinking about problems worth solving.
01
EdTech · Browser
A reading layer that closes the passive vocabulary gap

Tracks which words you look up or encounter repeatedly without using in your own writing. Surfaces contextual definitions on hover. Resurfaces terms across different texts over time — moving words from recognised to reachable as a byproduct of normal reading.

The gapKindle's vocabulary builder is disconnected from the browser. No tool closes the passive-to-active gap as a byproduct of reading rather than a separate task.
02
Fintech · Productivity
Subscription manager that connects cost to actual use

Maps all active subscriptions to the accounts they are linked to, tracks renewal dates, flags unused services, and surfaces true monthly cost. Banks show transactions, not patterns.

The gapMost people do not know what they pay monthly. No product connects subscriptions to actual usage data in one place.
03
EdTech · AI
Real-time lesson generator for the gap you are stuck on right now

Takes whatever topic a student is blocked on mid-session and generates a micro-lesson paced to their current understanding — not a fixed curriculum, not a pre-recorded video.

The gapExisting EdTech teaches to the syllabus. None adapt to the specific gap a student hits mid-session.
04
Media · AI
News reader that shows how a story got here

Maps stories as connected threads rather than a feed — showing how a story evolved, which sources framed it differently, and what context is missing from the headline you clicked.

The gapNews feeds optimise for recency, not understanding. No product shows how a story connects to its own history.
05
AI · Productivity
Digital twin trained on what you actually write and say

A personal AI model trained on your writing patterns, vocabulary, note-taking habits, and communication style — producing outputs that sound like you, not an AI approximation of you.

The gapCurrent AI assistants produce generic output regardless of who is prompting. A twin trained on your actual language would close that gap.
06
Productivity · Chrome
Account-to-service tracker for people with too many Google accounts

A Chrome extension that tracks which Google account is linked to which service, flags account conflicts before login friction hits, and gives a clear map of your multi-account digital life.

The gapManaging multiple Google accounts across dozens of services is a daily frustration with no dedicated tooling.

04 — Writing
Thinking out loud
1 piece published · more coming
Medium · EdTech · Product Critique · 2025
E-books were supposed to fix reading. They just moved the problem.
First published piece — tags: Product Design, UX, Reading, EdTech, PM
"The friction that makes people drift from e-books toward AI summaries has less to do with ideas being too hard and more to do with hitting an unknown word mid-sentence and facing two bad options: skip it and lose precision, or leave the page and lose the flow."

This piece argues that passive vocabulary is a product problem, not a literacy one. It describes a reading layer that does not yet exist — one that tracks vocabulary through behaviour, surfaces contextual definitions without breaking flow, and closes the passive-to-active gap as a byproduct of reading rather than a separate habit to build.

Term coined in this piece
Lexical debt
Vocabulary gaps that compound quietly over time through passive reading — recognised often enough to skip, understood rarely enough to use correctly.
